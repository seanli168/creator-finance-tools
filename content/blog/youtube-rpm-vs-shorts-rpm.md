# YouTube RPM vs Shorts RPM

Many creators notice that Shorts generate far more views than long-form videos.

But the revenue often feels surprisingly low.

That’s because Shorts RPM works very differently from traditional YouTube monetization.

---

# Long-Form Videos Usually Earn More

Traditional YouTube videos can display:

- Pre-roll ads
- Mid-roll ads
- Display ads

This creates more monetization opportunities per viewer.

Long-form educational content often produces the highest RPM.

---

# Shorts Use A Shared Revenue Pool

Shorts monetization works through a creator revenue-sharing system.

Ads appear between Shorts in the feed.

Revenue is then distributed across creators based on overall Shorts engagement.

This usually results in lower RPM.

---

# Typical RPM Differences

Many creators report numbers like:

| Content Type | Typical RPM |
|---|---|
| Long-form videos | $3-$20+ |
| Shorts | $0.01-$0.20 |

The difference can be massive.

---

# Why Creators Still Use Shorts

Despite lower RPM, Shorts are extremely powerful for growth.

They can help creators:

- Reach new audiences
- Gain subscribers quickly
- Increase channel visibility
- Drive traffic to long-form videos

Many successful creators use Shorts as a growth tool rather than a direct income source.

---

# Audience Quality Still Matters

Shorts targeting high-income countries may still earn more.

Advertiser demand continues to affect monetization performance.

---

# Long-Term Strategy Matters

Many creators combine:

- Shorts for growth
- Long-form videos for revenue

This hybrid approach is becoming increasingly common.

---

# Final Thoughts

Shorts RPM is usually far lower than traditional YouTube RPM.

However, Shorts can still play a major role in audience growth and long-term monetization strategy.
