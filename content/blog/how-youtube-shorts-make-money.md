# How YouTube Shorts Make Money

When YouTube first launched Shorts, many creators were confused about monetization.

Today, Shorts can generate real income, but the system works very differently from traditional long-form videos.

---

# Shorts Use Revenue Sharing

Unlike normal YouTube videos, Shorts ad revenue comes from a shared revenue pool.

Ads appear between Shorts in the feed.

Revenue is then distributed across creators based on total Shorts views.

---

# Shorts RPM Is Usually Lower

Most creators notice that Shorts RPM is much lower than long-form content.

That’s because:

- Shorts are shorter
- Fewer ads are shown
- Viewer sessions move very quickly

A Shorts creator may get millions of views but still earn less than a long-form creator.

---

# Views Still Matter

Although RPM is lower, Shorts can still generate income through massive reach.

Some creators use Shorts to:

- Grow subscribers
- Drive traffic to long videos
- Build brand awareness
- Increase sponsorship opportunities

Many channels combine Shorts with long-form content.

---

# Audience Location Still Matters

US traffic usually earns more than lower-income regions.

Audience quality still affects monetization performance.

---

# Shorts Can Help Grow Faster

One major advantage of Shorts is discoverability.

New creators often gain views much faster with Shorts compared to traditional videos.

That growth can later increase overall channel revenue.

---

# Sponsorships Matter More For Shorts Creators

Because Shorts RPM is lower, many creators focus heavily on sponsorships and affiliate marketing.

Brand deals often become a major income source.

---

# Final Thoughts

YouTube Shorts can absolutely make money, but creators should understand that Shorts monetization works differently from traditional videos.

Many successful creators use Shorts as part of a larger monetization strategy rather than relying on Shorts revenue alone.
