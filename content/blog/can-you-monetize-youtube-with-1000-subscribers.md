# Can You Monetize YouTube With 1000 Subscribers?

Many new YouTube creators believe reaching 1,000 subscribers is the final step toward making money.

But is having 1,000 subscribers enough to monetize a YouTube channel?

The short answer is:

**Yes, 1,000 subscribers can qualify you for YouTube monetization, but subscribers alone are not enough.**

You also need to meet additional YouTube Partner Program requirements.

---

# YouTube Monetization Requirements

To earn advertising revenue from YouTube, creators need to join the YouTube Partner Program (YPP).

The main requirements for full monetization include:

## Requirement 1: 1,000 Subscribers

Your channel must have:

- At least 1,000 subscribers

This shows that your channel has built a minimum audience.

---

## Requirement 2: Watch Time Or Shorts Views

You also need one of these:

### Option 1: Long-Form Videos

- 4,000 valid public watch hours within the last 12 months

### Option 2: YouTube Shorts

- 10 million valid Shorts views within the last 90 days

---

# Does YouTube Pay You When You Reach 1,000 Subscribers?

No.

Reaching 1,000 subscribers does not automatically generate income.

Subscribers are only one requirement.

YouTube earnings begin after:

1. You meet YPP requirements
2. You apply for monetization
3. You are approved
4. Ads are enabled on your videos

# How Much Money Can A 1,000 Subscriber YouTube Channel Make?

The number of subscribers does not directly determine earnings.

A channel with 1,000 subscribers can earn:

| Monthly Views | Possible Earnings |
|---|---|
| 10,000 views | $20 - $200 |
| 50,000 views | $100 - $1,000 |
| 100,000 views | $200 - $2,000+ |

Income depends mainly on:

- RPM
- Audience location
- Content niche
- Monthly views
