# How Do YouTubers Get Sponsored?

A lot of new creators assume sponsorships only happen after hitting huge subscriber numbers.

That’s not really true anymore.

Many small creators get sponsored if they have the right audience.

---

# Brands Care About Audience Quality

Subscriber count matters less than most people think.

Brands usually care more about:

- Audience trust
- Engagement
- Niche relevance
- Viewer demographics

A small finance channel can often get better sponsorships than a huge meme page.

---

# What Sponsors Look For

Companies want creators whose audiences are likely to buy products.

That’s why certain niches perform much better for sponsorships:

- Finance
- Tech
- Fitness
- Business
- Beauty

These audiences are valuable to advertisers.

---

# How Creators Usually Get Their First Sponsorship

Most creators get their first deal through:

- Email outreach
- Affiliate programs
- Brand marketplaces
- Inbound emails

Sometimes brands contact creators directly after videos start ranking in search.

---

# You Don’t Need Millions Of Views

This surprises a lot of people.

A creator with:

- 10,000 subscribers
- Strong engagement
- A focused niche

can absolutely land sponsorship deals.

Especially in business or software niches.

---

# Evergreen Videos Help A Lot

Search-based videos often attract sponsors because they continue getting traffic long-term.

For example:

- Product reviews
- Tutorials
- Comparisons

These videos have commercial intent.

Brands love that.

---

# Sponsorships Often Pay More Than Ads

Many creators eventually earn more from sponsors than YouTube ads.

That’s why audience trust becomes extremely important.
