# What Is CPM On YouTube?

CPM stands for Cost Per Mille.

It measures how much advertisers pay for 1,000 ad impressions on YouTube.

CPM is one of the most important advertising metrics for creators and marketers.

---

# How CPM Works

Advertisers bid to display ads on YouTube videos.

The amount advertisers are willing to pay depends on:

- Audience quality
- Niche
- Country
- Seasonality

Higher advertiser competition usually increases CPM.

---

# Average CPM By Niche

| Niche | Estimated CPM |
|---|---|
| Gaming | $2-$8 |
| Entertainment | $1-$5 |
| Technology | $5-$15 |
| Business | $10-$30 |
| Finance | $15-$50 |

Finance channels usually attract the highest CPM rates.

---

# CPM vs RPM

Many creators confuse CPM and RPM.

The difference is simple:

- CPM = advertiser spending
- RPM = creator earnings

RPM is always lower because YouTube takes a percentage of ad revenue.

---

# Why CPM Changes

CPM often fluctuates based on:

- Q4 holiday advertising
- Audience country
- Economic conditions
- Advertiser demand

Most creators notice higher CPM during the holiday season.

---

# Final Thoughts

Understanding CPM helps creators choose better niches and optimize monetization.

High CPM niches can dramatically increase YouTube income.
