# YouTube CPM By Country

One of the biggest factors affecting YouTube revenue is audience location.

Creators with mostly US viewers often earn dramatically more money than channels with traffic from lower-income countries.

---

# What Is CPM?

CPM stands for Cost Per Mille.

It refers to the amount advertisers pay per 1,000 ad impressions.

Higher advertiser demand usually means higher CPM.

---

# Countries With High CPM

Some countries consistently generate strong advertiser spending.

Examples include:

- United States
- Canada
- United Kingdom
- Australia
- Germany

These audiences are valuable because advertisers know viewers are more likely to spend money.

---

# Countries With Lower CPM

Lower-income regions often generate lower advertising rates.

That doesn’t mean creators can’t succeed there, but revenue per view is usually lower.

---

# Why Advertisers Pay More

Advertisers care about customer value.

Finance, software, insurance, and business companies often spend heavily in wealthy markets.

That directly affects CPM.

---

# RPM Is Also Affected

Higher CPM usually leads to higher RPM.

However, RPM is still lower because YouTube takes a percentage of ad revenue.

---

# English Content Often Earns More

Many international creators target English-speaking audiences because advertiser demand is stronger.

This is especially common in finance and business niches.

---

# Audience Quality Matters

Even within the same country, audience quality can change CPM significantly.

Advertisers usually pay more for:

- Business audiences
- Professionals
- Buyers
- High-income viewers

---

# Final Thoughts

YouTube CPM varies heavily depending on audience country and advertiser demand.

Creators targeting high-value markets often earn much more revenue from the same amount of views.
