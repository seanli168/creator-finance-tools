# What Is YouTube Shorts Monetization?

YouTube Shorts monetization allows eligible creators to earn money from short-form videos.

Unlike traditional YouTube videos, Shorts revenue comes from advertisements shown between videos in the Shorts feed.

The advertising revenue is collected and distributed among eligible creators.

---

# YouTube Shorts Monetization Requirements

To earn money from Shorts, creators need to join the YouTube Partner Program (YPP).

The main requirements include:

## Full Monetization Access

Creators typically need:

- 1,000 subscribers
- 10 million valid Shorts views within 90 days

This unlocks:

- Shorts ad revenue sharing
- Long-form ad revenue
- Additional monetization features

---

## Lower YPP Entry Level

Some creators can access limited monetization features with:

- 500 subscribers
- 3 public uploads within 90 days
- Either:
  - 3,000 watch hours
  - 3 million Shorts views

This level provides access to some fan funding features.

---

# How Does YouTube Shorts Revenue Sharing Work?

Shorts monetization is different from normal videos.

The process:

1. Ads appear between Shorts videos.
2. Revenue goes into a creator pool.
3. Music licensing costs are deducted.
4. Remaining revenue is distributed to creators.
5. Creators receive their share based on views.
