# Why Is My YouTube RPM Low?

Many creators become frustrated when their YouTube RPM is lower than expected.

Even channels with decent view counts sometimes generate very little revenue.

Fortunately, low RPM is usually caused by a few common factors.

---

# What Is RPM?

RPM stands for Revenue Per Mille.

It measures how much money a creator earns per 1,000 views after YouTube takes its revenue share.

RPM includes:

- Ad revenue
- YouTube Premium revenue
- Memberships
- Super Chats

---

# Common Reasons For Low RPM

## Low Value Niches

Some niches naturally attract lower advertiser spending.

Examples include:

- Memes
- Gaming highlights
- Viral clips
- General entertainment

Finance and business channels typically earn much higher RPM.

---

## Audience Location

Viewers from countries with lower advertiser demand usually generate lower RPM.

US, UK, and Canadian traffic often produces the highest revenue.

---

## Short Videos

Short videos generate fewer ad opportunities.

Longer videos can include mid-roll ads, increasing monetization.

---

## Poor Watch Time

Low audience retention reduces advertiser value.

Creators with strong watch time often earn higher RPM.

---

# How To Improve RPM

## Focus On Evergreen Content

Tutorials and educational videos usually monetize better.

---

## Target Search Traffic

Search-based videos often attract higher quality audiences.

---

## Improve Video Length

Videos longer than 8 minutes can include additional ads.

---

## Enter Higher RPM Niches

Business, finance, and software content usually perform better financially.

---

# Final Thoughts

Low RPM is common for new creators.

Instead of focusing only on views, creators should focus on audience quality and advertiser demand.

Over time, RPM usually improves as channels grow.
